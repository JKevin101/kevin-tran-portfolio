Syringe Pump

Overview

This syringe pump project is designed to deliver precise volumes of liquid at controlled flow rates using a lead screw mechanism powered by a stepper motor. It is an affordable alternative to commercial syringe pumps, utilizing 3D-printed components and Arduino-based electronics. Applications include laboratory microfluidics and clinical infusion scenarios.

Video Demonstration

Design Description & Iteration

The device features a stepper motor driving a lead screw, moving a carriage that presses the syringe plunger. Linear bearings on smooth rods ensure stable, smooth motion. Designed to be compatible with both 10 mL and 20 mL syringes, the slots in the carriage and rear support securely hold either syringe type.

A latching button toggles operation, while two momentary buttons allow manual plunger adjustments. An RGB LED indicates operational state—green (running), yellow (paused), and red (empty). A limit switch stops motion when the syringe is empty. Components are housed in a protective 3D-printed enclosure.

Parts List

Component Details
Nema 17 Stepper Motor Provides controlled linear motion
Lead Screws (2 mm & 8 mm) Translates motor rotation to linear motion
Linear Rods (8 mm) Guides the carriage smoothly
LM8UU Bearings Enables low-friction movement on rods
Flexible Coupler Connects motor shaft to lead screw
Arduino Uno Controls logic and motor steps
A4988 Stepper Driver Interfaces Arduino with stepper motor
Limit Switch Stops motion when syringe is empty
Latching Push Button Toggles start/pause function
Momentary Push Buttons Manually adjusts syringe carriage
RGB LED Indicates operational state
Power Supply Provides 5V, 12V, and 24V outputs
Breadboard & Wires Facilitates circuit connections
3D Printed Components Includes housing, carriage, and mounts
LCD Display Shows flow rate and time remaining

Conclusion

This syringe pump demonstrates a successful implementation of precision liquid delivery using accessible materials and fabrication methods. It shows how rapid prototyping, open-source electronics, and 3D printing can be integrated to create a fully functional, reliable, and cost-effective alternative to commercial devices.
