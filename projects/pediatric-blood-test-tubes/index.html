Pediatric Blood Tube - EZ Draw

Overview

The EZ Draw is a pediatric blood collection tube engineered to match adult tube dimensions for automation compatibility while ensuring a reduced blood draw volume of just 1 mL. The conical inner shape and resin-printed material allow for accuracy, affordability, and seamless integration into existing lab workflows. This project was part of our senior design capstone.

Design Process & Testing

Multiple prototypes were developed using different fabrication methods—initial filament printing, vacuum forming, and ultimately resin printing for its precision and effectiveness. All designs preserved adult-tube external geometry while altering internal volumes. The final medium design reliably drew 1 mL of fluid and integrated with existing vacutainer caps.

Initial Iteration – Inner geometry smaller than adult tubes.

Manufacturing & Industry Impact

Current pediatric blood tubes often cannot be processed on standard automated lab assembly lines due to their smaller dimensions. This requires manual aliquoting, slowing down workflow, increasing labor costs, and increasing the risk of error. EZ Draw solves this problem by matching adult tube external geometry while drawing only 1 mL—allowing it to integrate seamlessly with automation systems. This eliminates the need for manual processing and increases efficiency, reliability, and patient throughput in diagnostic settings.

Designs & Iterations

The development of EZ Draw involved several stages of material and process experimentation:

  • PLA: Used for early prototypes to explore internal geometry and wall thickness, but not airtight.
  • Nylon: Maintained shape well but was porous and failed to hold fluids reliably.
  • PET: Attempted due to its medical relevance, but 3D prints had weak layer lines and poor sealing.
  • Vacuum Forming: Worked for external shapes, but internal geometry lacked structural integrity.
  • Resin Printing: Provided airtight seals, detailed internal features, and structural strength—selected for final design.

Prototype Molds

To support our testing and scaling goals, we created several prototype molds to experiment with different manufacturing approaches:

  • PLA Vacuum Mold: A basic mold used to visualize tube geometry and evaluate vacuum forming viability.
  • Resin Mold: Used during vacuum forming trials, offering more detail and better surface finish for shape control.
  • PLA Injection Mold: Simulated an injection molding setup, giving insights into future scalable production methods.

Key Features

  • Draw Volume: Precisely 1 mL
  • Material: Resin (not medical-grade PET)
  • Compatibility: Works with standard automation and adult vacutainer caps
  • Applications: Pediatric blood diagnostics, low-volume clinical workflows
  • Advantages: Reduced patient discomfort, minimized aliquoting, increased workflow efficiency

Linear Actuator System

To simulate a clinical vacuum seal process and eliminate manual pressurization, a linear actuator was integrated into our test setup. The actuator was placed inside a sealed vacuum container and programmed to remotely press the cap onto the EZ Draw tube. This allowed consistent and repeatable vacuum application without breaking the container's seal, emulating how the tubes would be capped in an automated lab setting.

The actuator mechanism included a custom 3D-printed mount and plunger system to apply vertical force. Control was achieved through an external interface, keeping the internal environment sealed and stable during repeated trials.

Conclusion

EZ Draw represents a significant advancement in pediatric blood collection. Our work focused on reducing invasiveness while ensuring lab compatibility, all within limited resources. While the final prototype used resin, future iterations will explore regulatory-grade PET or similar materials to bring EZ Draw closer to clinical deployment.
