Bike Phone Mount Holder

Overview

This project focuses on the design and fabrication of a phone mount holder for a bike or scooter. The holder allows for quick attachment and secure rotation between portrait and landscape orientations without requiring tools. It incorporates a detent mechanism to provide tactile feedback for rotation and ensures the phone remains securely gripped while riding.

Design Process & Functionality

Designed using a top-down approach beginning with the bike mount, this project integrates the phone holder and dual detent mechanisms to enable smooth rotation between portrait and landscape orientations. The mount features a hinge system for easy installation and combines ball bearings and springs to securely snap the phone in place, offering both stability and tactile feedback.

Material Breakdown

SLS nylon was chosen for structural components like the mount and detent mechanisms, while PLA was used for the phone holder. A rubberized grip enhances adhesion to the bike handlebar and keeps the phone secure.

Manufacturing & Assembly

Prototyping was done using FFF with the Creality K1C, while the final version was printed using SLS nylon for durability. A tolerance of 0.15 mm was applied to ensure secure but smooth-fitting parts. Assembly includes screws, nuts, ball bearings, and springs‚Äîrequiring only one screw to lock the mount in place, allowing tool-free rotation.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates the effectiveness of combining modular 3D printing with mechanical design for everyday applications. By leveraging multiple materials, detent mechanisms, and top-down modeling, the bike phone mount offers both ease of use and structural reliability.
